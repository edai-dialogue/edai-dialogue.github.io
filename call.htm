Call for Papers

We invite submissions of 4-6 pages (references not included) of original papers at the intersection of early developmental learning and embodied intelligence, including but not limited to the following topics:

  • Embodiment: how the brain processes and integrates sensory and motor information, computational and AI models of sensorimotor learning, adaptation, and control; development and application of embodied AI and robotics inspired by early cognition and movement; how embodiment influences cognitive development in children and AI models that mimic developmental processes.
  • Learning Dynamics: mechanisms and computational models of neural plasticity, dynamics of training and adaptation in deep neural networks; data-driven dynamical system analysis; brain imaging studies on learning dynamics; time-dependent learning processes balancing exploration and exploitation in learning algorithms, and optimization.
  • Brain-inspired Systems: brain connectivity and connectomics and its role in shaping brain dynamics and function, neural network architectures inspired by brain connectivity; brain-machine interfaces and applications in rehabilitation and enhancement; neuromorphic computing and spiking neural networks; ethical and societal implications.
